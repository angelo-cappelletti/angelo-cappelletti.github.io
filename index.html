About Me:

My name is Angelo Cappelletti, I am 23 years old, and I hold a Bachelor’s degree in Mathematics from the University of Bologna. I am currently a Master’s student in Theoretical Mathematics at the University of Pisa.

Mathematics:

My main academic interests lie in pure mathematics, particularly commutative algebra and algebraic geometry, with a focus on topics such as moduli stacks and the computation of their invariants.

Finance & Investing:

I have been interested in personal finance and investing since I was 15 years old, and I have been studying this field continuously ever since. In fact, this interest was one of the main reasons that led me to study mathematics. I am particularly interested in corporate finance and equity investing, and I consider myself a strong advocate of value investing. On my own, I have studied financial statement analysis, corporate finance, and Modern Portfolio Theory. I am also interested in deepening my knowledge of business management and private equity, and I do not exclude the possibility of working in these areas in the future, even though my academic background is primarily quantitative.

In this area, I manage my own personal equity portfolio.

Quantitative Finance & Systematic Trading:

I am also very interested in quantitative finance and systematic trading. I have explored these topics independently by studying trading, technical analysis, and empirical market behavior, gradually moving towards more rigorous quantitative approaches. My studies include derivatives such as futures and options, classical pricing models, elements of stochastic modeling, volatility dynamics, and the design and validation of trading algorithms. Over time, I have also developed an interest in time series analysis, risk management, portfolio construction, and the practical aspects of trading strategy implementation and execution.

In this context, I manage a portfolio focused on statistical spread trading in commodities, based on seasonality and implemented in a fully systematic and mechanical way.

Personal Interests:

In my free time, I enjoy reading, watching movies, fishing, painting, spending time in nature, and being with good friends. I like travelling whenever possible, and I’m always interested in learning new things and experiencing new activities.

Bachelor’s Thesis — Hurwitz Theory: From Representations to Tropical Geometry

My Bachelor’s thesis focused on Hurwitz theory, which studies the enumeration of maps between Riemann surfaces with fixed discrete invariants.
